Combat ISP's Spying on Your Data!

Since enabling this extension, you have sent fake requests to your ISP.

Overview

This plugin is pretty simple. For every request your browser makes to a website, two additional calls to random websites are generated. These websites are generated using common words and phrases. They may be real, popular websites, or they may be completely made-up by the randomizer. Over time the randomizer will get better and we'll be able to pollute the data stream with even more nonsense. If they're going to take our data, let's at least make the information less useful.

How does it work?

Behind the scenes, without opening any browser tabs or interrupting your browsing activities, the extension will listen for outbound requests to websites. When a request is detected, it will generate two more random URL's and make requests at the same time for those two pages. You will never see the content and it doesn't mess with your browser history, so your experience will be just like normal.

What the ISP's will see however is a bunch of traffic going to sites that you may or may not have actually visited or have any interest in at all. By adding noise to your browsing data, the ISP's will have no idea which sites you're actually visiting, making the data they steal less valuable.

How can I see it working?

Well, it's not really straightforward, but you can watch the traffic to see the requests if you really want to do so:

  1. Go to "Window > Extensions"
  2. Scroll to the "Subhref" item and click on the "Background Page" link by the word "Inspect Views".
  3. This will open an instance of the Google Inspector.
  4. Click on "Network" then check the "Preserve log" checkbox
  5. Leave that window open and go to any other browser tab
  6. Enter a new URL and press "Enter"
  7. Return to the "inspector" window and you will see a bunch of requests going out to randomly generated websites.
This will show you a list of all of the websites being accessed in the background as you browse.

Are you collecting this data?

No! Your browsing data is not captured, logged or transmitted to any third-party server or service by this extension. In the future anonymous data might be sent in to a remote server in order to facilitate "real url" network noise generation, but if and when that happens it will be opt-in only and will be completely anonymous!
